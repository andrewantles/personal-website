Notes:

I've decided to review each line of code for several reasons, even though I know this is slowing me down considerably. Now, in the competitive world of augmented software development, time is money, and I can't afford to review every little line - that's what the coding assistant is there for. However, I'm able to drag several critical experiences and personal development opportunities out of this exersize. I'm understanding and exploring: - TDD - Data science, simulation, data gathering, feature engineering, etc. - Game design and general OOP program architecture - General front-end application development - Intricate analysis of LLM capabilities and tendencies - building intuition for moving at higher speeds later I think of it like doing reps or performing practice drills before the big game. I'm building the mental muscles and the fundamentals that will allow me to move faster, with great confidence, and with greater efficiency when the clock is running. The beautiful thing about the LLM is that an entire Internets-worth of the patterns (TDD, data science, OOP arch., front-end app dev, etc.) have been baked into it. When the LLM is producing the tokens based on statistical probabilities and relationships between tokens, these relationships and probabilities are derived from decades of suubject matter expert content. I get to study for free. Sure, I could go take a class or a course on any of these topics, OR I can dive right in and examine and experiment first hand with an entire Internets-worth of these concepts being manifest right before me.

Ok, enough romanticism and philosiphizing about LLMs and self-education.
